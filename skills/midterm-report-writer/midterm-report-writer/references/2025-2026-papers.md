# 2025–2026 Papers — Strategic Integration Guide

## Why Include These Papers?

Including recent 2025–2026 papers in your mid-report does three things:
1. **Shows academic currency** — you're aware of the latest developments
2. **Strengthens your motivation** — newer threats justify your research
3. **Positions your future work** — recent methods become your next experiments

**Rule:** Don't just mention them — use them for a specific purpose.

## How to Integrate Without Overwhelming the Report

### Strategy: "Mention but Don't Explain Deeply"

For papers outside your core 10, keep citations to **one sentence each**:

```
❌ (Too much) "The AFSS method by Nguyen-Le et al. proposes a self-synthesis 
approach that generates pseudo-fake samples using dynamic frequency swaps, 
time domain manipulations, and background noise augmentation to enforce 
learning of dataset-invariant features..."

✅ (Right amount) "Recent work on artifact-focused training [I] achieves a 
mean EER of 5.45% across seven datasets, motivating our future exploration 
of artifact-centric augmentation."
```

### Placement Map

| Lit Review 2.4 | [A],[B],[I] | Show 2025 generalization approaches |
| Future Work | [E],[F],[G],[H],[I] | Specific next-step motivations |

### Max Recommended: 4–5 recent papers in mid-report

For a mid-report, cite **3–5 of these papers maximum** — enough to show awareness, not so many that it reads like a survey update. Recommended picks:

1. **[G]** (LLM-based TTS) — for Introduction motivation
2. **[A]** or **[B]** (generalization 2025) — for Literature Review
3. **[C]** (SSL layer analysis) — for Methodology justification
4. **[F]** (2026 SOTA EER) — for Future Work benchmark target
5. **[I]** (AFSS) — for Future Work augmentation direction

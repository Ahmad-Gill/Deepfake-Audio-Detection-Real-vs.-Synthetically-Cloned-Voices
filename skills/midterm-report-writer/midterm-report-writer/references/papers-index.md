## PAPER 4: End-to-End Anti-Spoofing with RawNet2 (2020)
**arXiv:** 2011.01108 | **Authors:** Tak et al. | **Venue:** ICASSP 2021

**Role:** Primary baseline — implement this first

**Pre-written citable claims:**
- "Tak et al. [4] introduced the first end-to-end anti-spoofing system operating directly on raw waveforms, adapting the RawNet2 architecture with learnable sinc filters and Feature Map Scaling (FMS) attention."
- "RawNet2 [4] achieves an EER of 5.13% on the ASVspoof 2019 LA evaluation set, outperforming handcrafted feature baselines while operating without any manual feature engineering."
- "The RawNet2 architecture [4] comprises a bank of sinc-parameterized filters followed by six residual blocks, a GRU layer for temporal modelling, and a fully-connected scoring head, with the full system processing 4-second (64,000 sample) waveform segments."
- "An ablation study in [4] confirmed that temporal modelling via GRU and FMS attention both contribute meaningfully to detection performance, with removal of either component degrading EER."

**Architecture components for methodology:**
- Sinc filters → 6 residual blocks with FMS → GRU → FC → score
- Input: 64,000 samples (4 sec at 16kHz)
- Loss: weighted CE, Adam lr=1e-4

## PAPER 5: AASIST (2021)
**arXiv:** 2110.01200 | **Authors:** Jung et al. | **Venue:** ICASSP 2022

**Role:** Target/proposed architecture

**Pre-written citable claims:**
- "AASIST [5] proposes a heterogeneous stacking graph attention layer (HS-GAL) that jointly models artefacts in both spectral and temporal domains through a graph-based neural network."
- "Jung et al. [5] report an EER of 0.83% on ASVspoof 2019 LA eval, outperforming the previous state of the art by 20% relative, without relying on score-level ensembles."
- "Even the lightweight AASIST-L variant [5], with only 85K parameters, achieves 1.32% EER, outperforming all competing single systems in the 2021 challenge baseline comparisons."
- "AASIST's stack node and max graph operation [5] introduce a competitive selection mechanism that enables the model to focus on the most discriminative artefacts across heterogeneous temporal and spectral subgraphs."

**Architecture for methodology:**
- Input: raw waveform → RawNet encoder → heterogeneous graph
- Graph: spectral nodes + temporal nodes + heterogeneous GAT
- Output: max graph pooling → readout → score

## PAPER 8: Investigating SSL Front Ends for Spoofing (2021)
**arXiv:** 2111.07725 | **Authors:** Wang and Yamagishi | **Venue:** Odyssey 2022

**Role:** Justifies your SSL layer choice and fine-tuning strategy

**Pre-written citable claims:**
- "Wang and Yamagishi [8] demonstrate that pre-trained SSL models used as front-ends for spoofing countermeasures, when fine-tuned end-to-end, significantly outperform traditional LFCC features, achieving relative EER reductions of 68% and 79% on ASVspoof 2021 LA and DF respectively."
- "The study [8] finds that a deep LSTM-based backend (LLGF) combined with a fine-tuned wav2vec 2.0 XLSR front-end yields the lowest EER, outperforming shallow backends despite their simplicity."
- "Fine-tuning the SSL front-end with the anti-spoofing backend [8] is critical: freezing the SSL model and training only the backend yields substantially worse performance, suggesting that anti-spoofing artefacts require task-specific adaptation."

**Key finding to cite:** fine-tuning SSL + deep backend = best results
